About New Mexico Recipes

New Mexico Recipes was created by Fran Roybal on August of 2015. Coming from a large family of nine, Fran would watch her mother cook all of these wonderful New Mexico dishes and was always willing and eager to learn from her. Fran created the Facebook group New Mexico Recipes to bring people together to share the love for New Mexico Cuisine. You will find a lot of authentic recipes and photos from people all over the world in the group.

Feel free to contact us or submit your own recipes through the contact form at the bottom of this page or through the New Mexico Recipes Facebook group.

Blue Corn Pinon Pancakes

1 cup of flour
1/2 cup blue corn meal
1 tsp baking powder
2 tsps sugar
1/2 tsp salt
1 egg
3 tbsp melted butter (cooled)
1 1/4 cup cold milk
1/2 cup pinon nuts
Combine dry ingredients in mixing bowl. Add egg, butter and milk and whisk until combined. Do not over mix.
Heat griddle and butter place tbsp of pinon on griddle and pour batter to cover. Sprinkle additional pinon on batter and flip to cook on both sides. Serve with syrup or apple sauce.

About 2 lbs boneless skinless chicken cut into bite sized pieces
1 onion chopped
¬Ω bunch celery chopped
1 sweet red pepper chopped (I had a handful of multi colored minis which worked)

Red Chile

1/2 cup Pure Chimayo Red Chile powder
2 1/2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
2 or 3 Tbs. canola oil
1 small white onion, finely chopped
2 cloves fresh garlic, finely chopped
1 tsp. cumin seed, toasted and ground
1 tsp. Mexican oregano
1 tsp. salt
Put the Chimayo Chile powder in a medium bowl & whisk 1 cup of the broth into the powder to make a smooth mixture with no lumps, then set aside. Heat the oil in a large, heavy saucepan and saute the onion for 5 minutes over medium heat. Toss in the garlic and saute another 2 minutes. Stir in the cumin, oregano and cook, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes. Scrape the Chile mixture into the pan & stir; then add the remaining broth and cook, stirring, until the sauce reaches the simmering point. Do not let the Chile sauce scorch or boil. Reduce the heat to low, add the salt, and simmer the sauce gently, stirring frequently, for 20 to 30 minutes or until it is the consistently you like; then set aside to cool. When cool, pour into a clean jar, cover tightly, and store in the refrigerator. The sauce keeps well for up to 2 weeks. To keep longer, freeze it as soon as it has cooled. This recipe makes about 2 cups.

3 Eggs plus 2 egg whites
1 Cup Sugar
¬Ω Cup Pi√±on Nuts

1 8 Ounce Can Sweetened Condensed Milk
1 8 Ounce Evaporated Milk

Pre-heat oven to 350¬∞ F, place folded tea towel at the bottom of a 9 inch X 13 inch casserole dish (six ramekins should fit in the casserole dish without touching). Set aside. Horchata

1 cup of white rice
1 1/3 cup sugar adjust depending on how sweet you want (2/3 cup of sugar if you want it less sweet.)
1/2 cup chopped almonds
1 cinnamon stick
1 Tsp vanilla
1 Can 12 ounces Evaporated milk
1 1/2 cup of milk or almond milk
1 Liter of water
Ice
Start by soaking the rice, cinnamon, and almonds in a bowl of water all night, or at least for 5 hours so that the rice softens slightly. Strain the water from the cinnamon, rice, and almond mixture that were soaking, disposing of water. Blend the cinnamon, rice, and almond mixture with evaporated milk until a smoother mix is formed and the grains of rice are completely ground. Strain the resulting liquid into a pitcher, and add the sugar, vanilla, and milk. Mix well until everything is well combined. Add a liter of water, and serve with ice.
Enjoy!
