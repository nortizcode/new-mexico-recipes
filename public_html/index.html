New Mexico Recipes



About New Mexico Recipes

New Mexico Recipes was created by Fran Roybal in August of 2015. Fran is the daughter of Jose Ben and Mary Z. Roybal. Coming from a large family of nine, Fran would watch her mother cook all of these wonderful New Mexico dishes and was always willing and eager to learn from her. Fran created the Facebook group New Mexico Recipes to bring people together to share the love for New Mexico Cuisine. You will find a lot of authentic recipes and photos from people all over the world in the group.

Feel free to contact us or submit your own recipes through the contact form at the bottom of this page or through the New Mexico Recipes Facebook group.

Breakfast

Blue Corn Pinon Pancakes

1 cup of flour
1/2 cup blue corn meal
1 tsp baking powder
2 tsps sugar
1/2 tsp salt
1 egg
3 tbsp melted butter (cooled)
1 1/4 cup cold milk
1/2 cup pinon nuts
Combine dry ingredients in mixing bowl. Add egg, butter and milk and whisk until combined. Do not over mix.
Heat griddle and butter place tbsp of pinon on griddle and pour batter to cover. Sprinkle additional pinon on batter and flip to cook on both sides. Serve with syrup or apple sauce.

Cinnamon Muffins

2 cups all-purpose flour
1 cup sugar
4 teaspoons baking powder
1 tablespoon cinnamon
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 cup milk
1/2 cup vegetable oil
2 eggs
2 tablespoons sugar
1 teaspoon cinnamon
Preheat the oven to 375. Grease 16 muffins cups. In a bowl, combine the flour, sugar, baking powder, cinnamon and salt. In another bowl, beat together the milk, vegetable oil, and eggs. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and add the wet ingredients. Stir just until combined – don’t overmix. Divide the mixture evenly between the prepared muffin cups. In a small bowl, combine the 2 tablespoons sugar and 1 teaspoon of cinnamon. Sprinkle the cinnamon sugar mixture over the tops of the batter filled cups. Bake the muffins until a toothpick instead in the center comes out clean, 13-15 minutes.

Red Chile

1/2 cup Pure Chimayo Red Chile powder
2 1/2 cups vegetable or chicken broth
2 or 3 Tbs. canola oil
1 small white onion, finely chopped
2 cloves fresh garlic, finely chopped
1 tsp. cumin seed, toasted and ground
1 tsp. Mexican oregano
1 tsp. salt
Put the Chimayo Chile powder in a medium bowl & whisk 1 cup of the broth into the powder to make a smooth mixture with no lumps, then set aside. Heat the oil in a large, heavy saucepan and saute the onion for 5 minutes over medium heat. Toss in the garlic and saute another 2 minutes. Stir in the cumin, oregano and cook, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes. Scrape the Chile mixture into the pan & stir; then add the remaining broth and cook, stirring, until the sauce reaches the simmering point. Do not let the Chile sauce scorch or boil. Reduce the heat to low, add the salt, and simmer the sauce gently, stirring frequently, for 20 to 30 minutes or until it is the consistently you like; then set aside to cool. When cool, pout into a clean jar, cover tightly, and store in the refrigerator. The sauce keeps well for up to 2 weeks. To keep longer, freeze it as soon as it has cooled. This recipe makes about 2 cups.

Submitted by Pamela Lujan

Meat Empanaditas


1 Pound Beef Tongue, boiled, peeled and cut into long lengths for grinding
1 Pound Pork or Beef roast, cooked and cut into long lengths for grinding
1 Cup Piñon Nuts, shelled
½ Cup Granulated Sugar & ½ Cup Piloncillo or Dark Brown Sugar
3 Apples, baked or 1 ½ Cups Apple Sauce
½ Cup Golden Raisins
1 Tablespoon Ground Cinnamon
1 Teaspoon Ground Cloves, Vanilla, Sea Salt
1 Cup Broth, reserved from roast
1 Cup Cooking Oil for frying
Feed meat through meat grinder using disk for a medium grind. Add the rest of the ingredients except for the broth and piñon nuts and combine well. Depending on the texture of the filling add broth a little at a time or applesauce until the filling is desired consistency. Don’t make it too wet or your pastry will get soggy and hot oil will pot up when you are frying but you do not want a texture that is too dense either. Add piñon nuts at the end and fold into mixture until evenly distributed. Set aside. If you do end up with a filling that is too wet – put it uncovered in an oven pre-heated to 325° and bake for 20-30 minutes stirring once. Let filling cool before you assemble empanaditas.
PASTRY INGREDIENTS
6 Cups Flour
4 tsp. Baking Powder
2 tsp. Salt
1 ¼ Cups Lard or unsalted butter
¾ Cup Cold milk
Mix dry ingredients together and cut in lard or butter. Add just enough milk to make into a dough ball. Rest the dough in the fridge for at least 15 minutes before rolling. Roll dough out into a rectangular sheet and cut 4” rounds. Fill and fold pastry over into a half moon. Brush edge with damp brush and pinch edges to seal. Deep fry in hot oil a minute or two on each side until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.

Natillas

2 eggs, separated
1 1/4 cup of flour
1 pint of milk
3/4 cup of sugar
1/8 teaspoon salt
1/8 teaspoon cinnamon
Make paste with egg yolk, flour, and 1 cup of milk. Set aside. Add sugar and salt to remaining milk scald at medium heat. Add egg mixture and cook at medium temperature until reaches consistency of soft custard (approx. 30-40 minutes) Beat egg whites until stiff and fold into custard. Pour into serving dish, let it cool, sprinkle with cinnamon and serve.

Pinon Flan

3 Eggs plus 2 egg whites
1 Cup Sugar
½ Cup Piñon Nuts
1 Teaspoon Grand Marnier Liquor
1 8 Ounce Can Sweetened Condensed Milk
1 8 Ounce Evaporated Milk
1 ½ Teaspoon Vanilla
Tea Kettle of Boiling Water for hot water bath. Pre-heat oven to 350° F, place folded tea towel at the bottom of a 9 inch X 13 inch casserole dish (six ramekins should fit in the casserole dish without touching). Set aside. In a cast iron skillet over medium heat melt sugar until fully liquefied and cook until golden brown. Add Grand Marnier to sugar, stir and pour equally into ramekins swirling to coat bottom and sides. Add on tablespoon or so of piñon nuts to each ramekin and place on tea towel. Set aside. In a large bowl, beat eggs and yolks with milks and vanilla until smooth. Place in container with a spout and pour equally into the ramekins. Place in center of the oven and pour boiling water into casserole dish covering halfway up the ramekins, bake for 55 minutes or until custard has set firm but centers still jiggle. Cool completely overnight in the fridge. Run a knife around the edge before turning out.
Makes 6 servings.

Submitted by Liddie Martinez

Horchata

1 cup of white rice
1 1/3 cup sugar adjust depending on how sweet you want (2/3 cup of sugar if you want it less sweet.)
1/2 cup chopped almonds
1 cinnamon stick
1 Tsp vanilla
1 Can 12 ounces Evaporated milk
1 1/2 cup of milk or almond milk
1 Liter of water
Ice
Start by soaking the rice, cinnamon, and almonds in a bowl of water all night, or at least for 5 hours so that the rice softens slightly. Strain the water from the cinnamon, rice, and almond mixture that were soaking, disposing of water. Blend the cinnamon, rice, and almond mixture with evaporated milk until a smoother mix is formed and the grains of rice are completely ground. Strain the resulting liquid into a pitcher, and add the sugar, vanilla, and milk. Mix well until everything is well combined. Add a liter of water, and serve with ice.
Enjoy!


Submitted by Nathan Ortiz
